A lot of people arrive feeling like they keep ending up in the same argument — sometimes with the same person, sometimes with someone new who somehow triggers the exact same feeling. It can feel discouraging, like nothing ever really changes. But there's usually a reason it repeats, and once it's visible, it becomes much easier to work with.

## Patterns aren't random

We tend to learn our earliest scripts for closeness, conflict, and trust in our first relationships — often long before we have the language to notice it happening. Those scripts don't disappear; they just keep quietly running in the background, shaping how we react when we feel unheard, criticised, or unsure of where we stand.

## A gentle way to start noticing

You don't need to overhaul anything to begin. Just start noticing, without judgement, when a familiar feeling shows up — the tightening in your chest before a difficult conversation, the urge to withdraw, the same accusation forming on your tongue. Naming it in the moment ("this is the pattern") is often the first real step toward loosening its grip.

## Understanding isn't blame

None of this is about blaming yourself, or the people who shaped those early patterns. It's simply about understanding *why* things play out the way they do — because that understanding is usually what makes change possible. You can't shift something you can't see clearly yet.
