When anxiety takes over, it usually starts in the mind — but the fastest way out is often through the body. Here are three grounding techniques I share often, because they're simple enough to use anywhere, including in the middle of a hard moment.

## 1. Five things

Name five things you can see, four you can hear, three you can touch, two you can smell, one you can taste. It sounds almost too simple, but it works by giving your attention somewhere concrete to land, instead of looping on the same worry.

## 2. Slow the exhale

Anxious breathing tends to be shallow and fast. Try breathing in for four counts and out for six — the longer exhale is what tells your nervous system it's safe to settle. A few rounds is usually enough to feel a shift.

## 3. Cold water

Splashing cold water on your face or holding something cold triggers what's sometimes called the "dive reflex" — it genuinely slows your heart rate. Useful when things feel like they're moving too fast to think clearly.

None of these fix the underlying anxiety on their own — that's slower, steadier work, and it's exactly what therapy is for. But they can help you get through the next five minutes, which is sometimes all you need.
